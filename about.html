About

The Colours of Dance Academy (previously known as Chen Ling Academy of Dance) was established on September 1st, 2002 located in Richmond, BC. The Academy offers various levels of classes specializing in the diversity of Chinese dance and is proud to promote the excellence in the Chinese performing arts in Canada.

Colours of Dance Academy offers a comphrehensive range of Traditional, Folk, Classical, Modern and Minority styles of Chinese dance reprotoires for all ages. If you’re interested in learning more about our dance academy, we welcome you to send us an email or give us a call on your convenience.

Our Mission

The Academy is dedicated to providing high quality Chinese dance education for all of its students, while creating a unique avenue to learn about Chinese culture. Using the Chinese Dance Examinable Syllabus as the foundation of our teaching program, we strive to develop and maximize each student’s potential through the integration of modern teaching methods and professional standards.

Students in the Colours of Dance Academy are locally trained with the same strict standard used in China and our Academy also provide extensive training for students who would like to seek careers in the Faculty of Dance. Outstanding students would also have opportunity to study dance in China. Morever, students are encouraged to push beyond technical skills, as dance is pivotal to developing teamwork and community bonds. To gain confidence on the stage and outside of the studio, students are given opportunities to showcase their skills at performances both locally and around the world.

Ms. Chen, Ling 陳玲

Founder, Principal, Artistic Director

Chen Ling is a First State Rank performer, a well-known choreographer and a dance educator. She graduated and taught at the Beijing Dance Academy. She was the principal dancer in the classical chinese dance drama “ Xiao Dao Hui” and the ballet “Red Army Bride”. She has also performed numerous solos and leading roles that were highly received.

Individually and collectively, Chen Ling choreographed numerous dance dramas, song and dance dramas and dances. More than ten of her works were made into movies and some have been featured on televison specials. In addition, her works have been listed in the “Chinese Dance Dictionary”.

Chen Ling has taught dance in Vancouver for over 15 years. Her students performed well in North American Chinese Dance competitions such as Surrey Festival and Pacific West Dance Festival. In May 2002, her student Carmen Leung won gold at the “Golden Lotus International Friendship Cup” in Yunan, China, for which Chen Ling received the coaching award. In May 2005, Chen Ling choreographed the children dance drama “Dragon Dance” for the Chinese Cultural Center.

Chen Ling is active in volunteer work. She is founder and artistic director for “Chinese Performing Arts Society of Canada “, a charity dedicated to promoting Chinese dance in Canada. She organized a charity dance performance to raise funds for victims of the Sichuan earthquake. She gave volunteer dance classes to seniors at S.U.C.C.E.S.S, a charity dedicated to assisting new Canadians of Chinese descent to overcome cultural barriers.

Our Instructors

Ms. Jasmine Zhang 張珺

Vice-Principal, Instructor, Choreographer

Jasmine Zhang started full time dance training at age 13 at the prestigious Zhejiang Art School, a school dedicated to training students for careers in dance. She obtained her B.A. in Chinese Dance from internationally acclaimed Beijing Dance Academy, with a speciality in dance education. Following graduation, she taught full-time at Zhejiang Art School.

Jasmine is recipient of many academic honours, and awards at various nation-wide competitions. She won silver at the 6 province, East China dance competition. She graduated at the top of her class from Zhejiang Art School, and received guaranteed admission to Beijing Dance Academy (offered to 1 student per year). She won gold at the Beijing Student Chinese dance competition.

Jasmine was selected to perform on behalf of China and the Beijing Dance Academy at performances around the world including: the “Thousands of Hands and Eyes” in Malaysia; tour performances with the Youth Dance Group of Beijing Dance Academy in Germany; the International Dance Festival at the University of Connecticut, U.S.A. and the Hong Kong Hand-over Ceremony in Beijing, China. In 2008, Jasmine co-directed her solo dance performance, “The Beauty of Jasmine”, where she demonstrated her proficiency in several dance categories including modern, ballet, Chinese classical, Chinese folk, and original choreography. Moreover, Jasmine was very honoured to be awarded with an “Outstanding Women Award” in BC 2015 and a Taoli World Dance Competition “Outstanding Instructor Award” in 2018.

Ms. Alicia Xue

Instructor

Alicia Xue has studied and graduated from the People’s Liberation Army Arts College in the Faculty of Dance. Alicia was a primary dancer in the Nanjing Frontline Song and Dance Troupe. She is also a very experienced instructor.

Throughout her dance career, she received numerous awards from renowned nation-wide competitions, including the “Outstanding Young Performer” award at the first National Taoli Cup dance competition, Third place in the fourth Military Region Arts Showcase, and Second place in the Frontline Arts showcase.
